About MIC

The Mithila International Centre (MIC) was established by a group of committed Maithil professionals, educators, cultural practitioners, researchers, and civic leaders who recognised that Mithila deserved an institution to nurture intellectual discourse, preserve cultural heritage, and address regional challenges.

History & Structure

MIC operates as a member-based non-profit institution under Fulkoka Foundation. Registered to promote dialogue and development across Mithila's diverse communities, MIC builds on the region's intellectual traditions to address contemporary challenges.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

MIC is a socially conscious, independent, and forward-looking organisation serving as:

  • A platform reflecting Mithila's rich cultural ethos.
  • A premier destination for informed conversations on regional issues.
  • An institution upholding high standards of governance.

Our Mission

MIC fosters intellectual activity, open dialogue, cultural preservation, and innovative solutions for Mithila's sustainable development. Our programs include research initiatives and policy explorations focused on the region's unique needs.

Strategic Goals

MIC advances this vision through:

  • Convening diverse Maithil thinkers & leaders.
  • Promoting inclusive dialogue on priorities.
  • Curating cultural events & knowledge repositories.
  • Building networks with allied institutions.
